Your Recommendations

What is the main goal of creating CAPRI?

CAPRI is our Conext-Aware Interpretable Point-of-Interest Recommendation Framework. As it provides and supports reproducibility of results, users can easily share their experimental settings to achieve the same outcomes for comparative experiments. This contains pre-implemented methodological workflows, a wide range of models and algorithms, and various datasets for benchmarking purposes.

What makes CAPRI unique compared to other recommendation frameworks?

In contrast to the introduced frameworks, CAPRI is only focused on the Point-of-Interest domain and tries to provide all the tools needed for this field.

What datasets can be found in CAPRI?

In the current version of the framework, we have provided modified versions of two popular check-ins datasets: Gowalla and YELP. Gowalla is collected by a location-based social networking website with the same name and covers user check-ins over the period of February 2009 to October 2010. YELP is the other dataset provided in CAPRI, containing data fetched from ten metropolitan areas across two countries.

What models can be found in CAPRI?

CAPRI covers the recent implementations of various models, which can be applied on the introduced datasets for evaluation and reproducibility goals. The models implemented in the current version are GeoSoCa, LORE, and USG.

What evaluation metrics are supported in CAPRI?

CAPRI supports a variety of evaluation metrics and evaluation methodologies. The metrics established in the configuration file are executed over the recommendation results. We hereby divide the evaluation metrics of into two main categories: Accuracy (precision@k, map@k, etc.) and Beyound Accuracy (Novelty, Personalization, etc.).
